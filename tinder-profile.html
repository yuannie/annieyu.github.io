Annie Yu WorkAbout Resume
At Tinder, I worked on improving users' profile viewing and profile editing experience.
IDEATION
I began sketching as many ideas on how we could surface our users' personalities as possible.
We felt that increasing profile customization and leveraging profile completion would best communicate a user's personality.
IDEA 1—info arrange
Allow users to arrange pre-existing information: their bio, instagram, top spotify artists in a way that's meaningful to them.
Version 1—drag + icon

A higher-fidelity prototype of the intial sketch. Users could drag their profile content modules to arrange them and determine what textual information would overlay on their photograph.

I added icons on each photo to inform users what content was assigned to which photo. This iconography was echoed in the modules for consistency.
Version 2—drag + photos

Unfortunately, my assumption that icons would communicate the link between the content modules and photographs was false. People who tested the feature didn't notice the iconography moving and one person didn't know how to interact with the hamburger icon.

In response, I replaced the icons with the photos themselves and changed the hamburger menu to up-and-down arrows to indicate vertical interaction.
FINAL VERSION—photos + content adding

After further testing, the overlay on the photos was removed because it unnecessarily obscured the photograph. Without it, the user's photos could be seen clearly.

The up/down arrows caused people to tap on the module rather than drag, so I brought back the hamburger menu since that had more success before, and added the ability to edit content in the modules.
IDEA 2—Add content
Encourage users to add content to their profiles because fuller profiles are more successful.
Version 1—Redirect to add

Users can preview their own card when they enter profile settings.

Users can see how they look to other people—which wasn't an available feature previously, and can tap on a large CTA on their photos which informs them of missing information and then redirects them to complete those parts of their profile.
FINAL VERSION—Add on card

After interacting with the feature myself and watching others do so, I saw that redirecting users up and down the screen was too disorienting, especially if they had many incomplete parts of their profile.

So I chose to let users edit directly on the card instead. This final version in comparision felt like less work for the user.
